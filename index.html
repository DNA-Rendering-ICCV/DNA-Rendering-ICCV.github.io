DNA-Rendering

A Diverse Neural Actor Repository for High-Fidelity Human-centric Rendering

Get Started

Abstract

We present DNA-Rendering, a large-scale, high-fidelity repository of neural actor rendering represented by neural implicit fields of human actors. We provide human subjects with

    Grand categories of pose actions, body shapes, clothing, accessories, hairdos, object intersection
    Richly-annotations including 2D/3D human body keypoints, foreground masks
    SMPL-X models
    60 synchronous cameras, max 4096 × 3000 resolution, 15fps

Highlight Data

Our dataset contains 500 actors and covers a wide range of special performances and everyday actions, including a large variety of costumes, makeup, and interaction object types.

Scales

To our knowledge, our dataset far surpasses similar ones in terms of the number of actors, costumes, actions, clarity, and overall data volume.


Annotation Pipeline

Our dataset comes with off-the-shelf high-precision annotation, including 2D/3D human body keypoints, foreground masks, and SMPL-X models. We have implemented targeted optimization methods to achieve high accuracy in our results.

Rendering Benchmark

Our dataset supports rendering-based novel view synthesis tasks. Most current human rendering methods can handle easy and medium difficulty levels, but still lack the capabilities to model the hard ones.

Amimation Benchmark

Additionally, our dataset also supports novel pose animation tasks.
